My Journey in Life

The Art of Well-Being

Today, I was listening to Radio Hamrah when a young woman called to ask about her marriage, and how her partner insisted on a prenup. For those who don't know, a prenuptial agreement, commonly referred to as a prenup, is a written contract you and your spouse enter into before getting legally married. It details exactly what happens to finances and assets during your marriage and, of course, in the event of divorce. Although in some cases, a prenup can help, most of the time can be destructive. The funny thing is that those people don't understand that by marrying someone they share their life with someone, their children, and their moments. All the moments that could potentially hurt them. The question is "Why do you trust to share your life with someone, while you cannot share your property with them?" We as humans, like other animals have needs that should be satisfied, especially as Mazlo pointed out, our physiological needs are essential for our being. The second level is related to security and safety. If you satisfied those needs you can go to upper levels like love and belongings, self-esteem, and so on. Finally, you get to the point that satisfying the need is not essential for being but it improves the state of your well-being or becoming. Only the first level is about owning things, and you can satisfy your security need by owning more material. Self-actualization is the highest level that you and only you are worth. You think about yourself as something far more valuable than materials like your house, car, etc. We can simply understand that the person who does not pass the second level can not be in a state that can love and be loved. Although many of them do not agree with that. However, if you cannot trust someone with your money, your marriage is not at the third level and about love. You see your partner as an object to become more secure by having them. Such people have very low self-esteem. They don't love themselves and cannot believe that someone can love them because, in their point of view, they are worthless. Nobody. Nothing. Therefore, if someone comes close to them, it must be about what they own.

My Minimalism Philosophy

Since the summer of 2015, when I moved to California from Europe, I have moved into different homes several times. Almost every year, I had to change my place, and although I didn't own so much stuff, I was stressed out each time that I was supposed to move to a different house. I had to spend a lot of my time packing my thing and being worried about the big moving day. In my last move from Irvine to Sacramento, I decided to keep the things I need and get rid of the extra and unnecessary items. Later I became interested in learning about minimalism philosophy and lifestyle. As a minimalist, I can say I am at the starting point of my journey to live a simpler and happier life. So, what is minimalism anyway? There is no right or wrong answer to this question. Everyone has own interpretation of this concept. However, I define minimalism such that as a minimalist, I am removing the distraction of excess possessions so I can focus more on those things that matter most. Minimalism is all about freedom. Minimalism isn't an end point. It's a constant process of editing, revisiting, editing some more.

Economics

Economics is the science that concerns itself with economies. An economy is the large set of inter-related production, consumption, and exchange activities that aid in determining how scarce resources are allocated. Economics is a social science concerned with the production, distribution, and consumption of goods and services. It studies how individuals, businesses, governments, and nations make choices about how to allocate resources.

I believe everyone should be familiar with the basics of economics in the modern societies. I like studying economics because this knowledge gives you the power to understand how humans or societies deal with each other over having access and using the scare resources.

Read and Review a Book

Six Pillars of Self-Esteem

by Nathaniel Branden
My rating: 5 of 5 stars

The Six Pillars of Self-Esteem is a psychology book written by Dr. Nathaniel Branden. Dr. Branden is known as the "father" of the Self-Esteem movement and this book is his most popular work.



My Review:
Everybody should read this book, especially people with low self-esteem. Most psychological disorders such as depression, anxiety, and anger come from a lack of self-esteem. During a person's mental development, most babies between the age of one and three years old received millions of signals from other people regarding their weaknesses. Because children make mistakes and others correct them, they think they are bad. In the next stages of development, as what Piage introduced as the preoperational, concrete, and formal operational stages, the bad feeling of a child must be recovered by positive messages from parents, friends, and the educational system. Otherwise, the person could suffer from a low-esteem. In this book, Dr. Brandon provides a scientific and empirical method for building one's self-esteem as an adult.

Algorithms to Live By: The Computer Science of Human Decisions

by Brian Christian and Tom Griffiths
My rating: 5 of 5 stars

A fascinating exploration of how computer algorithms can be applied to our everyday lives, helping to solve common decision-making problems and illuminate the workings of the human mind.


My Review:
Decision-making is one of the most critical functions of our mind. In this book, the authors clearly show how peoples' minds are programmed. The whole idea of algorithm design is about how our minds process and the steps we use to overcome challenges. Psychology explains how humans' minds operate when it comes to tasks that are difficult but common. We need to solve a problem once by finding the proper steps, remember them, and use them any time we face the same or similar problems. This idea is how computers work. Computers are programmed to make our life easier by completing tasks faster as a result of the combination of the power of the mind and the functionality of microprocessors that use with multiple levels of software. Authors go over the most crucial concepts of data structures and algorithms like searching, sorting, optimizing, graphs, etc., and show how such ideas were born. Although the literature is fairly simple, smooth, and does not include too many technical words and phrases, its concepts can be somehow difficult for readers with no or limited background in computer science and mathematics.

Mapping and Taking Stock of the Personal Informatics Literature

Proceedings of the ACM on Interactive, Mobile, Wearable and Ubiquitous Technologies
December 2020 Article No.: 126

Abstract: The research community on the study and design of systems for personal informatics has grown over the past decade. To take stock of what the topics the field has studied and methods the field has used, we map and label 523 publications from ACM's library, IEEE Xplore, and PubMed. We surface that the literature has focused on studying and designing for health and wellness domains, an emphasis on understanding and overcoming barriers to data collection and reflection, and progressively fewer contributions involving artifacts being made. Our mapping review suggests directions future research could explore, such as identifying and resolving barriers to tracking stages beyond collection and reflection, engaging more with domain experts, and further discussing the privacy and ethical concerns around tracked data.

Paper Browser web application is developed specifically for this research. This website is a powerful tool for organizing, categorizing, and improving the study as it provides an opportunity for scholars to access the original references easily and quickly. It also lets the authors enrich the quality of the work by uploading their new publications.
Paper Browser - Department of Informatics, UC Irvine - June 2020
